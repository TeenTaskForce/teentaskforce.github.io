Tutorial: How To Make An Attractive Flyer

  1. First, choose a template that will suit the product/service/event you are advertising and fit all needed information.
  2. Choose a color palette that fits the mood and style of what is being advertised.
  3. Create a big, bold, eye-catching header that will attract the reader’s attention right away. The font should be unique and fancy but also legible. Note that the color of the text should fit the color palette and complement the rest of the flyer while still being the most striking part.
  4. List your text in strategic places based on the order of their importance. Also, the text that is more important should have a bigger font.
  5. Also make sure that the text is concise so the reader does not get bored reading it.
  6. To facilitate this for the reader, create designs and such that will act like visual “arrows” and point the reader to what they should read next.
  7. For the text, use a font that is easily readable.
  8. Adding a few visual aids, such as images, might also add to the flyer but make sure not to overdo it and add too many elements to the flyer. This might confuse the reader and he or she will not know where to focus. They will not feel like reading it.
  9. Finally, make sure every element is laid out properly. Make sure that all the colors are complementary and don’t distract from the main header. Make sure that the text is legible and has all the needed information. Make sure that the flyer is visually pleasing and will attract people, not drive them away.
  10. You now have an attractive flyer! Congratulations!

Tips For a Successful Pitch Presentation

  1. First of all, your pitch should be straightforward and concise as well as engaging and relatable.
  2. As soon as you meet your judges or whoever you are presenting your pitch to, shake their hands and manifest yourself as a confident, respectful person. Create a good impression of yourself in front of the judges and start off on a good note.
  3. When you start your speech, start strongly and confidently. Show that you are passionate through your facial expressions.
  4. Maybe tell the judges an anecdote to engage them and get them interested in your pitch.
  5. Establish that you are knowledgeable in the subject you are speaking about.
  6. Project well, but don’t shout. Enunciate your words and present clearly. Speak with inflection and enthusiasm.
  7. Speak naturally, but also be prepared on your topic. Note: Don’t use filler words such as “um” and “uh” or blabber randomly if you don’t know what to say next.
  8. Pace yourself when speaking; don’t speak too fast or too slowly. Make sure everyone has time to process and understand what you are saying.
  9. Keep a good posture and stand with poise. Use hand gestures to add to your speech.
  10. Use the stage to your advantage! Move around a little. You don’t have to stay stuck in the same place.
  11. Lastly, but most importantly, don’t forget to HAVE FUN!
